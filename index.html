Who Was He?

About Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

A visionary scientist, remarkable teacher, and one of India's most respected leaders who inspired generations to dream big and work hard for the nation's progress.

Missile Man of India

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Despite coming from a modest background, he pursued education with determination and became one of India's greatest aerospace scientists.

His contributions to India's space and missile programmes earned him the title "Missile Man of India." Beyond science, he was admired for his humility, simplicity, and dedication to inspiring young minds.

As the 11th President of India (2002–2007), he remained deeply connected with students and believed that education, innovation, and values were the foundation of a developed nation.

Scientist

Led major missile and aerospace programmes that strengthened India's defence and technological capabilities.

Teacher

Dedicated his life to motivating students and encouraging innovation through education and curiosity.

Bharat Ratna

Honoured with India's highest civilian award for his outstanding contribution to science and society.

Life Journey

Timeline of Excellence

From a small town in Tamil Nadu to becoming India's People's President, Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's life was a journey of perseverance, knowledge, and service.

1931

Birth

Born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, into a humble family that valued education and hard work.

1960

Scientist at DRDO

Joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and began his remarkable journey in aerospace engineering.

1969

Joined ISRO

Became Project Director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), marking a milestone in India's space programme.

1980

SLV-III Success

Successfully launched the Rohini Satellite into orbit, making India one of the few nations with satellite launch capability.

1997

Bharat Ratna

Awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour, for his exceptional contribution to science and technology.

2002

President of India

Elected as the 11th President of India and became widely known as the "People's President."

2015

Everlasting Legacy

Passed away while delivering a lecture to students, leaving behind a timeless legacy of education, innovation, humility, and patriotism.

Achievements

Honours & Contributions

Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam devoted his life to science, education, innovation, and nation-building. His achievements continue to inspire millions around the world.

Bharat Ratna

Received India's highest civilian award in 1997 for his outstanding contributions to science, technology, and national development.

Missile Programme

Led India's Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, strengthening the country's defence capabilities and earning the title "Missile Man of India."

Space Technology

Played a significant role in the successful launch of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), marking a milestone in Indian space history.

Inspiring Teacher

Continued interacting with students throughout his life, encouraging innovation, curiosity, leadership, and lifelong learning.

Best-Selling Author

Authored inspirational books including Wings of Fire, Ignited Minds, India 2020, and My Journey.

People's President

Remembered for his humility, simplicity, compassion, and unwavering commitment to the progress of India and its youth.
